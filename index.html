ABOUT



As a Computer Engineering Ph.D. advised by Robert LiKamWa in the Fulton Schools of Engineering at Arizona State University, I am committed to advancing the frontiers of human-computer interaction experiences using cutting-edge technologies such as mixed reality.

From my time at Meteor Studio, I aspire to leverage my knowledge and expertise to create transformative, immersive experiences for education and promote overall health and wellness.

My passion for these fields was sparked during my Bachelor's studies in Electrical Engineering at the University of Washington, where I graduated with Honors in 2018. While there, I had the privilege of conducting pioneering research alongside trailblazers Shwetak Patel and Hanchuan Li in the Ubiquitous Computing Lab.

RESEARCH FUNDING


  • Principal Investigator, VR Clubs of America

    Project Description: VR Club addresses the challenge of inadequately equipped classrooms and low-resource schools by pioneering virtual and augmented reality science labs. In underserved communities, where traditional lab equipment is scarce, this initiative harnesses augmented and virtual reality to provide immersive scientific experiences. Through these virtual labs, students can conduct experiments, visualize intricate concepts, and engage in interactive learning, all without physical apparatus limitations. This innovative solution not only overcomes resource constraints but also ensures equal access to quality education. We empower students to cocreate their own science educations and AI teachers , developing technology literacy and reinforcing science concepts. We envision educators using the experiences that the students have built in the classroom to empower other students learning.

  • Mentor, VR Brain, won by Micaela Leong

    Project Description: The "VR Brain" project tackles the challenge of limited student exposure to real brains until medical school and the spatial constraints of traditional textbook images. Utilizing Virtual Reality (VR) technology, the initiative provides students with an immersive, three-dimensional experience, addressing the shortcomings of conventional methods. By offering early access to neuroanatomy through dynamic VR simulations, VR Brain aims to bridge the knowledge gap, allowing students to explore and understand the complexities of the brain's structure and functionality in an interactive and comprehensive manner. This marks an advancement in neuroanatomy education towards a more engaging and accessible learning experience.

MEDIA RECOGNITION


Spot the Scammer

Scams are becoming more sophisticated, with Americans losing over $10 billion in 2023—a 20% increase from the previous year. Older adults and low-income individuals are particularly vulnerable, highlighting the need for better scam awareness. To address this, we developed and shipped Spot the Scammer, an iOS app designed to educate users on identifying and avoiding scams. As the project manager, I collaborated with a developer and designer to bring this app to life, aiming to empower at-risk communities with essential fraud prevention skills.

Komatsu & Meteor Studio: Virtual Reality Haptic Pre-Op Inspection Experience - Mining Truck Safety Training, Arizona State University

From summer 2022 to spring 2023, our team at Meteor Studio has worked to create a VR solution that will transform training for truck operator trainers in the mining industry. The vision is to optimize safety and efficiency, making the lives of truck operator trainers easier while transforming the industry for the better. As project lead, I worked with Komatsu Stakeholders and Meteor Studio students to create this experience - from the vision to implementation to iteration. We interviewed many operator trainers, collaborated with industry experts, and employed cutting-edge technology to craft an immersive VR environment that solves their mining challenges. The project culminated in Komatsu licensing our software for deployment, driving innovation in mining training.
